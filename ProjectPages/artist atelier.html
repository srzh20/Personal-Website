Artist Atelier

The concept behind Viatorem house is to separate the living space from the workplace in the same form. The word “Viatorem” is the Latin word for traveler; the house was named Viatorem because it contains a sort of journey within itself. When we commute to work our mindset changes and we think differently. Nick Dodge, the artist for whom Viatorem House is designed, keeps an emphasis in his life on always staying relevant and being a constant innovator. Without some sort of ritual to separate where we relax and sleep it can be difficult for anyone, especially an artist, to get into the right mindset to innovate and be creative. Viatorem House aims to encapsulate the ritual of a commute into the house; to distinguish the place of work from the place of leisure very clearly.

There are many distinctions between the living space and the workshop. The living space is low to the ground and closer to the water, it has places to relax and enjoy life as well as places to sleep. On the contrary, the workshop is very high in the mountains, overlooking the water, but not close in proximity, allowing the artist to be above the clouds, physically and mentally. The houses gesture at each other, as if in conversation, symbolizing the important of connecting work and play, while keeping them distinctly separate at the same time.
